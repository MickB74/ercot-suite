What it means

Solar capture decline in ERCOT is structural and accelerating, not a bad-weather blip. Every solar PPA struck against a fixed price is fighting a falling capture curve. Three implications for the book:

  • Forward capture must trend down, not flat. A model that freezes a recent ratio (e.g. 94%) is structurally optimistic — the realized curve is sliding ~5–10 pts/yr in spring.
  • Evening-firming assets gain. The flip side of the midday crash is a steeper evening ramp — batteries and wind that deliver after sundown capture a rising premium.
  • Node beats hub for siting truth. Congested solar pockets see the deepest midday basis; the asset's node, not the hub, tells you the real economics.

How the forward price & capture forecast is built

The projected bill combines a monthly price forecast with a per-asset capture model — the second is what turns a hub price into the dollars a specific plant actually earns. Here is each layer, and how summer scarcity and the price cap enter.

Monthly hub price× capture shape× generation− strike= net settlement

The four layers

1

Price level — monthly, per hub

Market-implied heat-rate × gas strip + Monte Carlo (the Eroct_forecasts engine) gives each hub a P10 / P50 / P90 around-the-clock price per month, out to the liquid gas horizon (~18 months); beyond that we repeat the seasonal shape. This layer is monthly, not hourly — there is no hourly gas strip to forecast against.

2

Capture shape — per asset, hourly

Capture price = generation-weighted price ÷ average price. It is calibrated from each plant's own metered SCED generation × settled price history: a realized seasonal curve (P50 by month), refined by the hour-of-day duck curve so it reflects when the plant runs vs. when price is high. Solar generates midday when prices sag, so its capture sits below 100%.

3

Forward trend — the deepening midday trough

ERCOT solar has grown 8× in five years, pushing midday prices from ~89% to ~54% of the daily average. So forward capture is not held flat — the midday belly is deepened ~4%/yr for solar (0 for wind), and the capture ratio erodes each year rather than freezing at today's level.

4

Scarcity — paired with the price band

Summer value is extreme-tail: the top 1% of Jul–Aug hours carry ~36% of all price, in the 7–8pm ramp. We can't put that in a monthly average, so it lives in the P90 band — the high-price (scarce-summer) scenario is paired with a scarce-day capture shape, so each price percentile gets the capture the asset actually achieves in that scenario.

The scarcity calibration uses a recent 3-year lookback and clips prices to the current $5,000 cap — so the scarce-summer scenario reflects today's regime and can never be inflated by a retired $9,000-era spike. Recent realized peaks (2024 $3,768, 2025 $2,038, 2026 $1,341) sit well under the cap.

Honest limits

  • Price level is monthly. What's hourly is the capture shape (when you earn), not hourly price discovery — there's no hourly gas strip.
  • Scarcity is in P90, not P50. It's a tail event; the expected bill (P50) shouldn't assume a scarce summer.
  • Band deltas are bounded. Three years of daily composites is noisy, so the scarce/mild capture swing is clamped to ±25% to keep a bad composite from ever producing a nonsense bill.
  • Node vs hub basis is shown separately — hub-settled contracts leave that basis with the generator.
